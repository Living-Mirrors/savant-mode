## Selection Methodology

Papers and books were selected according to four criteria:

1. **Direct relevance.** The work must address the cognitive architecture of dyslexic cognition — not remediation strategies, not reading intervention programs, not deficit-focused assessment. The question is: *what does the dyslexic brain actually do differently, and what are the measurable consequences of that difference?*

2. **Empirical grounding or theoretical primacy.** Each source is either (a) an empirical study with reproducible methodology, (b) a theoretical work that introduced a concept subsequently validated by empirical research, or (c) a foundational text that defined a research program. Popular science summaries and secondary literature were excluded.

3. **Independence.** The five research programs represented here were conducted independently — different decades, different fields, different methodologies. This independence is what makes their convergence significant. Sources were selected to represent genuinely distinct lines of inquiry, not a single tradition citing itself.

4. **Specificity of contribution.** Each source must provide something no other source in the collection provides. Redundant coverage was eliminated. Where multiple papers address the same phenomenon, the one with the strongest methodology or the most direct relevance to the specification was retained.

## Citation Format

All citations follow APA 7th edition format. DOI links are provided where available. For books, publisher and edition are specified. For journal articles, volume, issue, and page numbers are included.

In-text references throughout the research directory use author-date format: (Taylor & Vestergaard, 2022), (von Karolyi, 2001), (Eide & Eide, 2011).
