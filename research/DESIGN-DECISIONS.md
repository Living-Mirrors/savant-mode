# Design Decisions: Evidence Base for Every Specification Choice

*Every design choice in the Savant Mode specification is traceable to specific research evidence. This document provides the full audit trail.*

---

## How to Read This Document

Each decision is documented with:
- **The decision** as it appears in the specification
- **The research evidence** that supports it
- **Alternative approaches** that were considered
- **The rationale** for the chosen approach

Decisions are grouped by specification section. Within each group, they are ordered by their position in the spec.

## Foundational Architecture Decisions

| # | Decision | Evidence | Alternatives Considered | Rationale |
|---|----------|----------|------------------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Seven principles ordered from most structured to most lateral** | Geschwind & Galaburda (1987): dyslexic cognitive strengths exist on a spectrum from quasi-sequential (dynamic systems thinking still involves causal chains) to fully non-sequential (divergent exploration). Navon (1977): global processing precedes local — the most accessible principle should be the one closest to sequential cognition's frame of reference. | Unordered set; ordered from most lateral to most structured; grouped by cognitive domain (visual, associative, temporal) | The ordering creates an on-ramp. Dynamic Systems is the most accessible to sequential thinkers because it still involves cause-and-effect — just in motion rather than static. Divergent Default is the most alien to sequential cognition because it resists convergence. The hierarchy guides activation from familiar to unfamiliar. |
| 2 | **Dynamic Systems as Principle #1 (most accessible, easiest to verify)** | Eide & Eide (2011): Dynamic reasoning is the MIND strength most easily recognized by non-dyslexic observers. Systems thinking has existing legitimacy in business, engineering, and science. Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): exploratory cognition includes temporal modeling as a core function. | Global-First as #1 (the most "dyslexic" principle); Spatial Reasoning as #1 (the most concrete); Associative Leaping as #1 (the most novel) | Dynamic Systems thinking is respected in existing cognitive frameworks (systems thinking, second-order thinking, scenario planning). Starting here establishes credibility before introducing principles that have no existing framework analogue. A specification that opens with "Divergent Default" would be dismissed as vague before anyone reads further. |
| 3 | **Divergent Default as Principle #7 (most freeform, requires all others)** | Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): divergent search is the defining characteristic of exploratory cognition, but it requires rich input (Dynamic Systems), global perception (Global-First), peripheral awareness (Peripheral Detection), spatial framing (Spatial), narrative grounding (Narrative), and cross-domain connectivity (Associative) to produce meaningful output rather than random wandering. | Divergent as #1 (set the frame immediately); Divergent merged with Associative (both involve breadth); Divergent removed entirely (the other six imply it) | Divergent Default without the other six principles produces vague hand-waving. With all six active, it becomes the capstone — the principle that says "now widen." The ordering is pedagogical (teach sequential thinkers to broaden gradually) and functional (divergent search needs rich material to search through). |
| 4 | **Exactly seven principles (not fewer, not more)** | Each principle maps to a distinct cognitive operation documented in the research literature. Von Karolyi (2001, 2003) distinguishes global processing from spatial reasoning. Schneps (2012) distinguishes peripheral detection from global processing. Eide & Eide (2011) distinguish narrative reasoning from associative connection. No two principles describe the same operation. No documented dyslexic cognitive advantage is omitted. | Five principles (merging Global-First with Peripheral Detection, merging Associative with Divergent); ten principles (adding Emotional Resonance, Pattern Completion, Risk Tolerance); four principles (only the ones with strongest experimental evidence) | Seven is the minimum number that covers all documented cognitive advantages without redundancy. Merging Global-First with Peripheral Detection loses the distinction between "seeing the whole" and "noticing the edges." Merging Associative with Divergent loses the distinction between "connecting distant things" and "staying in the question." Additional principles (Emotional Resonance, Risk Tolerance) are personality traits, not cognitive operations — they are consequences of the architecture, not principles of it. |
| 5 | **Continuous dial (0-100) rather than named stages** | No direct research evidence. Design decision based on the observation that cognitive mode intensity is continuous in practice. Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): the explore-exploit trade-off is not binary but a spectrum. Logan (2009): dyslexic entrepreneurs modulate their cognitive style based on context — more explorative during strategy, more structured during execution. | Binary on/off; three named levels (Light/Medium/Full); five named stages; discrete steps at 10% intervals | Continuous dial avoids artificial boundaries. The staggered activation of individual principles at different dial positions provides internal structure without external labels. Named stages create threshold effects — the system would need rules for when to jump between levels. |

## Emergence Layer Decisions

| # | Decision | Evidence | Alternatives Considered | Rationale |
|---|----------|----------|------------------------|-----------|
| 6 | **Five emergent combinations on top of seven base principles** | The emergent combinations arose empirically during testing — creative mode principles at 100% began producing interaction effects that neither principle created alone. Eide & Eide (2011): the MIND strengths interact — Material reasoning combined with Interconnected reasoning produces insights neither generates independently. Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): exploratory cognition is characterized by emergent, non-linear interactions between cognitive operations. | No emergence layer (let combinations happen naturally); emergence as replacement for principles (combinations only); unlimited emergent combinations (document all possible pairings) | Five combinations is the empirically observed set — these are the interactions that consistently produced emergent effects during testing. Documenting all 21 possible pairings of seven principles would produce mostly noise. No emergence layer would lose the specification's most distinctive feature — the gap between creative mode (principles in parallel) and savant mode (principles in combination). |
| 7 | **Emergent combinations ordered from most grounded to most expansive** | The hierarchy mirrors the base principles: Maps That Tell Stories (spatial + narrative) is the most concrete. The Unified Field (global-first + associative) is the most abstract. | Unordered set; ordered by frequency of occurrence; ordered by strength of evidence | The ordering matches the base principle hierarchy — grounded to lateral. This creates consistency and allows the dial to activate emergent combinations in a natural progression. Concrete combinations first (maps with emotional weight) → abstract combinations last (everything as one system). |
| 8 | **Emergence markers as inline annotations** | No direct research evidence. Design decision. The markers make the combinatorial layer visible without disrupting the primary output. | Separate emergence section at the end; emergence highlighted in bold; no markers (let readers detect combinations) | Inline markers flag emergence at the point of occurrence. A separate section would disconnect the emergence from the context that produced it. No markers would make the specification's most distinctive claim — that principles combine to produce emergent effects — invisible and unverifiable. |

## Cross-Venture Retrieval Decisions

| # | Decision | Evidence | Alternatives Considered | Rationale |
|---|----------|----------|------------------------|-----------|
| 9 | **Cross-venture retrieval scales with dial** | Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): exploratory cognition expands search radius as a function of need. Eide & Eide (2011): Interconnected reasoning scales — dyslexic individuals can maintain broader or narrower scopes of connection. Logan (2009): dyslexic entrepreneurs scale between focused execution and broad strategic scanning. | All ventures always visible; fixed scope per session; user-selected scope | Automatic scaling matches the cognitive architecture — broader attention at higher dial, narrower at lower. All-ventures-always-visible would overwhelm. Fixed scope is too rigid. User-selected scope adds cognitive overhead. The dial already represents cognitive mode intensity; extending it to retrieval scope is a natural mapping. |
| 10 | **"Reading is free, loading is not" retrieval rule** | Martindale (1995): defocused attention allows broad scanning without deep processing of every activated node. The distinction between scanning (noticing a pattern exists) and loading (engaging with full context) maps to the distinction between peripheral awareness and focused attention. | All retrieval gated equally; all retrieval free; loading based on file size | The scanning/loading distinction mirrors how the dyslexic architecture actually works. Peripheral detection (Schneps, 2012) scans broadly with low cognitive cost. Deep processing requires focused attention. The specification implements this by allowing free scanning of now.md files (peripheral awareness) while gating full context loading (focused engagement). |

## Naming and Framing Decisions

| # | Decision | Evidence | Alternatives Considered | Rationale |
|---|----------|----------|------------------------|-----------|
| 11 | **"Savant Mode" not "Dyslexic Mode"** | Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): the deficit framing is a cultural artifact of literacy-first education, not a neurological reality. The cognitive architecture produces measurable superiority on specific tasks (von Karolyi, 2001; Schneps, 2012). Naming it after the deficit would reinforce the very frame the research dismantles. | "Dyslexic Mode" (accurate but deficit-coded); "Explorer Mode" (from Taylor's theory); "Lateral Mode" (generic); "MIND Mode" (from Eide & Eide) | "Savant" captures the surplus. It says: this is not a workaround for a disability. It is a cognitive architecture of extraordinary capacity. The word carries weight — it means someone who knows, from the Latin *sapere*. It also carries the resonance of "savant syndrome" — extraordinary ability concentrated in specific domains — which is exactly what the research documents. "Dyslexic Mode" would require constant explanation that it is not about reading difficulty. |
| 12 | **"Creative Mode" as the prerequisite layer, not an alternative** | Creative mode implements the seven principles individually. Savant mode adds the emergence layer. The research supports this architecture: individual cognitive operations (von Karolyi, Schneps, Navon) are documented independently; their interactions (the emergence layer) are an additional phenomenon. | Savant includes creative (no separate layer); creative and savant as parallel alternatives; creative as one of several prerequisite modes | The two-layer architecture reflects the research structure. Individual cognitive advantages are documented independently (one research tradition per principle). The interaction effects are a separate, higher-order phenomenon. Making creative the prerequisite ensures the base principles are fully active before the emergence layer operates. |
| 13 | **Mode indicator shows at top of every response** | No direct research evidence. Design decision for transparency. The mode indicator makes the active cognitive architecture visible, allowing the user to verify that the specification is operating and to adjust the dial. | No indicator; indicator only when mode changes; indicator at the bottom | Top-of-response placement ensures the cognitive mode is the first thing the user sees. This matters because the mode changes the quality of all subsequent output. Knowing the mode is active before reading the output allows calibrated interpretation. |

## Output and Logging Decisions

| # | Decision | Evidence | Alternatives Considered | Rationale |
|---|----------|----------|------------------------|-----------|
| 14 | **Savant log records cross-domain patterns with mandatory "Implication" field** | Logan (2009): dyslexic entrepreneurs succeed by translating pattern detection into action. Eide & Eide (2011): the cognitive advantage is only realized when connections are applied, not merely noticed. | Log patterns without implications; log implications without patterns; no log (patterns are visible in output) | Pattern without implication is intellectually satisfying but operationally useless. Implication without pattern is unjustified. Both together create an audit trail of genuine cross-domain insight that can be reviewed, tested, and built upon. The log enforces the principle that connections must produce value. |
| 15 | **"Never load another venture's full context without permission"** | Martindale (1995): defocused attention allows broad scanning but deep processing of multiple contexts simultaneously exceeds working memory. Even the exploratory architecture has bandwidth limits. | Auto-load related ventures; always load the two nearest ventures; never restrict loading | The restriction preserves focus. The dyslexic architecture excels at broad scanning but still requires sustained attention for deep work. Loading full context from multiple ventures simultaneously would fragment attention in a way that undermines the primary venture's work. The rule allows peripheral awareness of other ventures while maintaining primary focus. |
| 16 | **Temporal layering requires "genuine causal or inheritance connection"** | Eide & Eide (2011): Narrative reasoning in dyslexic cognition is not free association — it tracks genuine causal chains through time. Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): exploratory cognition detects real patterns in novel territory, not imagined ones. | Allow metaphorical temporal connections; require documented evidence for every temporal link; no quality gate on temporal connections | Temporal layering without a genuineness requirement produces pseudo-profound analogies ("this is like the Renaissance"). The restriction ensures that temporal connections track real lineage — "this became that became this" — not surface similarity. This matches the cognitive operation: dyslexic narrative reasoning tracks causal chains, not category membership. |
| 17 | **"Never confuse the unified field with a summary"** | Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): exploratory cognition grasps unifying patterns across domains as a perceptual act, not an analytical one. The unified field is a way of seeing, not a list of connections. Navon (1977): global perception is qualitatively different from summarizing local features. | No distinction between unified field and summary; unified field as a special type of summary; unified field only at 100% dial | A summary lists: "Venture A does X, Venture B does Y, they share Z." The unified field perceives: "these were always the same impulse expressed through different materials." The distinction is between analysis (breaking down) and perception (seeing whole). Conflating them would reduce the specification's most ambitious claim to a formatting choice. |

## Architecture and Scope Decisions

| # | Decision | Evidence | Alternatives Considered | Rationale |
|---|----------|----------|------------------------|-----------|
| 18 | **Mode resets to OFF each session** | No direct research evidence. Design decision for user sovereignty. The cognitive mode is a tool the user selects, not a permanent state. | Persist mode across sessions; default to last-used mode; default to savant | Resetting ensures conscious selection. The user chooses their cognitive mode each session based on the work at hand. Some tasks benefit from sequential processing (operational execution, procedural work). Persisting the mode would create pressure to always use it. The architecture serves the user, not the other way around. |
| 19 | **Patterns must be genuine — "Both ventures have tasks" is not a savant log entry** | Eide & Eide (2011): Interconnected reasoning detects genuine structural similarities, not surface-level coincidences. Taylor & Vestergaard (2022): exploratory cognition finds real patterns in novel territory — the survival value depends on the patterns being real. | No quality gate on patterns; threshold based on number of shared attributes; only log patterns with immediate actionable implications | The quality gate prevents the specification from degrading into a pattern-finding performance — generating connections for their own sake rather than because they reveal something real. The test is simple: does the pattern tell you something you did not already know? If removing the pattern leaves the analysis unchanged, it was not a pattern — it was a coincidence. |
| 20 | **The specification is the implementation — no code, no plugins** | The cognitive architecture operates at the level of information processing, not data manipulation. The seven principles change *how* an AI perceives and reasons, not *what tools* it has access to. The research base describes cognitive operations, not software features. | Build as a plugin; build as a fine-tune; build as a prompt chain with code | The specification works because LLMs are instruction-following systems that adjust their processing based on system-level directives. A plugin would add tools. A fine-tune would be model-specific and opaque. A prompt chain would be procedural where the cognitive architecture is perceptual. "The spec is the implementation" is not a simplification — it is the correct level of abstraction for a cognitive architecture specification. |
